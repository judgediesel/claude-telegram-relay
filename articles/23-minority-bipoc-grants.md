If you're a member of a racial or ethnic minority group in the United States, there are hundreds of grant programs designed specifically to help you succeed — in business, education, homeownership, healthcare, and more. These programs exist because systemic disparities in wealth, income, education, and access to capital have created gaps that standard government programs alone haven't closed.

The grants listed in this directory are real, verified programs that provide free money — not loans — to qualifying individuals and organizations. We've organized them by category and included specific details on award amounts, eligibility requirements, and how to apply. Whether you're a Black entrepreneur looking for startup capital, a Hispanic student pursuing a college degree, a Native American family seeking housing assistance, or an Asian American nonprofit serving your community, this guide will point you toward funding you may not have known existed.

**A note on terminology:** This article uses the terms "minority," "BIPOC" (Black, Indigenous, and People of Color), and specific racial/ethnic identifiers as they appear in each program's official eligibility criteria. We recognize that these terms don't fully capture the diversity of experiences within any group.

## Business Grants for Minorities and BIPOC Entrepreneurs

Access to capital remains one of the biggest barriers for minority entrepreneurs. According to the Federal Reserve's 2023 Small Business Credit Survey, Black-owned businesses are approved for financing at roughly half the rate of white-owned businesses, and when they are approved, they receive smaller amounts. These grants help bridge that gap.

### Grants for Black Business Owners

**1. The NAACP Powershift Entrepreneur Grant Program**
- **Amount:** $5,000-$25,000
- **Eligibility:** Black entrepreneurs with established or early-stage businesses
- **Focus:** Business development, expansion, and community impact
- **Website:** [naacp.org](https://www.naacp.org)

**2. Black Founders Fund (Google for Startups)**
- **Amount:** Up to $100,000 in non-dilutive funding
- **Eligibility:** Black-led startups in the U.S. and Canada
- **Focus:** Tech-enabled startups at the seed or Series A stage
- **Extras:** Google Cloud credits, mentorship, and technical support
- **Website:** [startup.google.com](https://startup.google.com/programs/black-founders-fund/)

**3. Backstage Capital**
- **Amount:** $25,000-$100,000
- **Eligibility:** Founders who are Black, Latinx, or women
- **Focus:** Underrepresented startup founders
- **Website:** [backstagecapital.com](https://backstagecapital.com)

**4. National Black MBA Association Scale-Up Pitch Challenge**
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000
- **Eligibility:** Black entrepreneurs with scalable business models
- **Focus:** Business growth and scaling
- **Website:** [nbmbaa.org](https://nbmbaa.org)

**5. Black Ambition Prize**
- **Amount:** $250,000 (grand prize), $75,000 (runner-up), $25,000 (additional awards)
- **Eligibility:** Black and Hispanic entrepreneurs
- **Focus:** Tech, healthcare, consumer products, and sustainability
- **Founded by:** Pharrell Williams
- **Website:** [blackambitionprize.com](https://www.blackambitionprize.com)

## Housing and Homeownership Grants for Minorities

Homeownership is the primary wealth-building tool for most American families, but minority homeownership rates significantly lag behind: the Black homeownership rate is approximately 45% compared to 75% for white Americans (U.S. Census Bureau, 2024). These programs help close the gap.

### Down Payment Assistance Programs

**35. HUD Section 184 Indian Home Loan Guarantee Program**
- **Amount:** Loan guarantees for home purchases on tribal trust land or in approved areas
- **Eligibility:** Enrolled members of federally recognized tribes
- **Focus:** Homeownership for Native Americans
- **Website:** [hud.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/ih/homeownership/184](https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/ih/homeownership/184)

**36. NACA (Neighborhood Assistance Corporation of America) Mortgage Program**
- **Amount:** Below-market interest rates, no down payment, no closing costs, no PMI
- **Eligibility:** Low-to-moderate income individuals; focuses heavily on minority communities
- **Focus:** Affordable homeownership
- **Website:** [naca.com](https://www.naca.com)

**37. State and Local Down Payment Assistance Programs**
- **Amount:** Typically $5,000-$25,000
- **Eligibility:** Varies by program; many are targeted at minority and low-income homebuyers
- **How to find them:** Visit [downpaymentresource.com](https://downpaymentresource.com) and enter your zip code
- **Focus:** Closing the homeownership gap

**38. Bank of America Community Homeownership Commitment**
- **Amount:** Up to $17,500 in down payment and closing cost assistance
- **Eligibility:** Low-to-moderate income borrowers in select markets (many are majority-minority communities)
- **Focus:** First-time homebuyers in underserved communities
- **Website:** [bankofamerica.com](https://www.bankofamerica.com/mortgage/community-homeownership-commitment/)

### Housing Rehabilitation and Repair Grants

**39. USDA Section 504 Home Repair Program**
- **Amount:** Up to $10,000 in grants (for homeowners 62+); up to $40,000 in low-interest loans
- **Eligibility:** Very low-income homeowners in rural areas (disproportionately minority communities)
- **Focus:** Home repair and safety improvements
- **Website:** [rd.usda.gov](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/single-family-housing-programs/single-family-housing-repair-loans-grants)

**40. Habitat for Humanity Programs**
- **Amount:** Below-market-rate home purchases and home repair programs
- **Eligibility:** Low-income families; local affiliates serve many minority communities
- **Focus:** Affordable housing construction and repair
- **Website:** [habitat.org](https://www.habitat.org)

## Healthcare Grants for Minorities and BIPOC Communities

Health disparities along racial and ethnic lines remain significant in the United States. These programs provide direct funding and resources to address those gaps.

### Individual Healthcare Assistance

**41. Indian Health Service (IHS)**
- **Amount:** Free or low-cost healthcare services
- **Eligibility:** Members of federally recognized tribes and their descendants
- **Focus:** Comprehensive healthcare including medical, dental, mental health, and substance abuse treatment
- **Website:** [ihs.gov](https://www.ihs.gov)

**42. National Health Service Corps (NHSC) Scholarship Program**
- **Amount:** Full tuition, fees, and a living stipend for medical, dental, and mental health students
- **Eligibility:** Students committed to practicing in underserved communities (Health Professional Shortage Areas)
- **Focus:** Primary care in underserved areas, which are disproportionately minority communities
- **Website:** [nhsc.hrsa.gov](https://nhsc.hrsa.gov)

**43. Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)**
- **Amount:** Sliding-scale healthcare services based on income
- **Eligibility:** Anyone, regardless of insurance status or ability to pay
- **Focus:** Primary care, dental, mental health, and pharmacy services
- **Find one near you:** [findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov)

### Community Health Grants

**44. CDC Racial and Ethnic Approaches to Community Health (REACH) Grants**
- **Amount:** Up to $750,000/year for community organizations
- **Eligibility:** State and local health departments, tribal organizations, nonprofits
- **Focus:** Reducing health disparities in minority communities
- **Website:** [cdc.gov/reach](https://www.cdc.gov/reach)

**45. HRSA Health Equity Grants**
- **Amount:** Varies by program; typically $100,000-$500,000
- **Eligibility:** Community health organizations serving minority populations
- **Focus:** Maternal health, chronic disease prevention, behavioral health
- **Website:** [hrsa.gov](https://www.hrsa.gov)

## Tips for Applying to Minority-Specific Grants

### 1. Document Your Eligibility Clearly

Many minority grant applications require documentation of racial or ethnic identity. This can include:
- Tribal enrollment card (for Native American grants)
- Self-identification on the application form
- Organizational documentation showing minority ownership (for business grants)
- Community involvement or service documentation

### 2. Start Local

Local and regional grants typically have less competition than national programs. Check with your city government, county economic development office, community foundation, and local chamber of commerce.

### 3. Apply for Multiple Grants Simultaneously

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Apply for several grants at once to maximize your chances. Many of these programs can be combined with each other and with federal financial aid.

### 4. Tell Your Story Authentically

Grant reviewers for minority-specific programs understand the challenges that minority communities face. Don't shy away from discussing how systemic barriers have affected you or your community. Be honest, specific, and connect your story to how the grant will help you overcome those barriers.

### 5. Meet Every Deadline

Competition for minority grants is fierce. Late applications are almost never considered. Set calendar reminders for every deadline and submit at least 2-3 days early to avoid technical issues.

### 6. Follow Up

After submitting, follow up to confirm receipt. After receiving a decision, ask for feedback whether you're funded or not. Build relationships with funders for future opportunities.

### 7. Leverage Free Help

Take advantage of free resources before hiring a consultant:
- MBDA Business Centers offer free grant-writing assistance for minority business owners
- SCORE mentors provide free business counseling (find one at [score.org](https://www.score.org))
- Many universities have grant-writing workshops open to the public
- Library systems often host free financial literacy and grant-writing programs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are minority grants only for U.S. citizens?

It depends on the specific program. Federal grants typically require U.S. citizenship or permanent residency. Some private foundation grants have more flexible eligibility. Always check the specific program's requirements.

### Can white individuals apply for minority grants?

Generally, no. These grants have specific eligibility criteria tied to racial or ethnic identity. However, many similar grant programs are available based on income, geographic location, gender, or other factors regardless of race.

### Do I have to prove my race or ethnicity?

Requirements vary. Some programs accept self-identification. Others require documentation such as tribal enrollment cards. Business grants may require certification as a Minority Business Enterprise (MBE) through your state or the National Minority Supplier Development Council (NMSDC).

### Can I receive multiple minority grants at the same time?

Yes. There is generally no restriction on receiving grants from multiple sources, as long as each program's rules allow it. In fact, stacking multiple grants is a smart strategy.

### Are these grants taxable?

It depends on the type. Education grants used for qualified education expenses (tuition, fees, books) are generally tax-free. Business grants are typically considered taxable income. Personal grants for specific purposes (housing, healthcare) have varying tax treatments. Consult a tax professional for guidance specific to your situation.

### Where can I get help writing my applications?

Free help is available through MBDA Business Centers, SBDCs, SCORE mentors, and many community organizations. For education grants, your school's financial aid office can often assist with applications. See the "Leverage Free Help" section above for specific resources.

## The Bottom Line

Hundreds of millions of dollars in grant funding is specifically earmarked for minority and BIPOC individuals, businesses, and communities every year. Whether you need money for education, starting a business, buying a home, or accessing healthcare, there are programs designed to help you.

The key is knowing where to look and investing the time to submit strong applications. Start with the programs listed in this directory that match your needs. Use the search tools and community resources we've outlined to find additional opportunities. Apply to multiple programs simultaneously. And don't give up — the funding is real, it's substantial, and it's specifically intended for people in your situation.

Every dollar you receive in grant funding is a dollar you don't have to borrow, repay, or go without. Take the time to explore these opportunities. The money is there. Go get it.
